Manifesto

Learn from Failure not from Success

Anything truly new is by definition unpredictable. So having the guts to jump in at the deep end and make a lot of mistakes, to experiment, play and prototype, is the only way to avoid failure in the long run. As developer, I have the tools, methods and techniques and not least — the attitude — it takes to facilitate a process like this and come out with a result that adds value.

Hold the Vision, Trust the Process

My work is contemporary, has a character of its own and creates a strong impression — but it is always based on my development process. This process means an analytical approach to the task that ensures real and measurable effects and adds value for both me and my users.

Nothing to Prove, Everything to Improve

Is it working? This is a daunting question and people often avoid asking it, because they are afraid of the answer. I'm not. Success comes only after continual evaluation of what works and what doesn't. Let's have the guts to be honest with ourselves and be willing to quickly change tactics if that's what it takes to achieve results.
